## Evolution of Contract Pharmacy Policy

### Pre-1996: No Contract Pharmacy Allowed
Initially, 340B drugs could only be dispensed through a covered entity's **in-house pharmacy**. If a covered entity did not operate a pharmacy, it could not benefit from 340B pricing.

### 1996 HRSA Guidance: One Contract Pharmacy
In 1996, HRSA issued guidance allowing covered entities that do not operate an in-house pharmacy to use **one contract pharmacy** arrangement. This provided limited flexibility for small entities.

### 2010 HRSA Guidance: Unlimited Contract Pharmacies
The major expansion occurred with HRSA's **2010 contract pharmacy guidance**, which allowed covered entities to have an **unlimited number** of contract pharmacy arrangements — regardless of whether they also had an in-house pharmacy.

This dramatically expanded 340B program reach, particularly for large health systems.

### 2020-2021: Manufacturer Restrictions
Beginning in **2020 and 2021**, several major pharmaceutical manufacturers (AstraZeneca, Sanofi, Eli Lilly, Novo Nordisk, and others) began imposing conditions on 340B contract pharmacy purchases — for example, limiting 340B pricing to one contract pharmacy per covered entity, or restricting 340B pricing to in-house dispensing only.

HRSA responded by:
- Issuing **advisory opinions** stating these restrictions violate the 340B statute
- Initiating the **civil monetary penalty (CMP) process** against certain manufacturers
- Asserting that 42 U.S.C. § 256b(d)(1)(B)(vi) (added by the ACA) authorizes CMPs for knowing and intentional overcharging — which HRSA argues includes denying access to the 340B price through restrictions

Federal courts have issued varying decisions in the ongoing litigation.

## Key Contract Pharmacy Requirements

Under the 2010 guidance, covered entities using contract pharmacies must:

1. Have a **written agreement** with each contract pharmacy
2. The agreement must include:
   - Patient confidentiality protections
   - Covered entity's **audit rights** over the pharmacy
   - Provisions ensuring prevention of diversion and duplicate discounts
   - Accountability for TPA data management
3. Register the contract pharmacy in **OPAIS** within 10 business days
