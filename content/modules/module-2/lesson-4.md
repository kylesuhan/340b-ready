## Covered Outpatient Drug Eligibility — Review

As covered in Module 1, a **covered outpatient drug** must be:

- FDA-approved (or DESI-effective)
- Dispensed on an **outpatient** basis
- Not an excluded drug type (OTC, inpatient, orphan drug for certain entities)

## The Orphan Drug Exclusion in Practice

Under the ACA, certain hospital types cannot use 340B pricing for **orphan drugs** — drugs designated for rare diseases.

**Affected entity types:**
- Children's Hospitals
- Free-standing Cancer Hospitals
- Critical Access Hospitals
- Rural Referral Centers
- Sole Community Hospitals

**Not affected:** FQHCs, Ryan White grantees, and other non-hospital grantee entities.

## Understanding the Carve-In / Carve-Out Decision

A covered entity's approach to Medicaid managed care impacts both compliance and financial strategy:

### Carve-Out (Common Default)
- Covered entity does **not** use 340B drugs for Medicaid MCO patients
- Avoids duplicate discount risk entirely
- **Downside:** Foregoes 340B savings on Medicaid MCO prescriptions

### Carve-In (Higher Complexity)
- Covered entity uses 340B drugs even for Medicaid MCO patients
- Must implement controls to prevent duplicate discounts
- Requires coordination with the MCO and state Medicaid
- **Upside:** 340B savings extend to the MCO patient population

## Documentation Requirements

Regardless of approach, the covered entity must maintain records demonstrating which prescriptions were purchased at 340B prices and which were not — especially when billing Medicaid.

Many covered entities rely on their split-billing/TPA system to generate reports showing:
- Which NDCs were purchased at 340B
- Which claims were billed to Medicaid
- How the covered entity ensured no overlap (no duplicate discount)
