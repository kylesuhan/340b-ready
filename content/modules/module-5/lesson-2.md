## Key Recent Developments (2020-2024)

### Manufacturer Contract Pharmacy Restrictions
As covered in Module 4, starting in 2020, multiple manufacturers restricted 340B contract pharmacy access. As of 2024, this remains an area of active HRSA enforcement and ongoing litigation.

**Key HRSA positions:**
- Restrictions violate 42 U.S.C. § 256b
- HRSA has CMP authority under § 256b(d)(1)(B)(vi) for knowing and intentional overcharging
- Patient data demands as conditions of 340B pricing are prohibited

### AHA v. Becerra (Supreme Court, 2022)
In **American Hospital Association v. Becerra**, the U.S. Supreme Court ruled **unanimously** that CMS violated the Medicare statute when it reduced reimbursement for 340B hospitals under Medicare Part B from ASP+6% to ASP-22.5% without first conducting the required survey of hospitals' acquisition costs.

**Impact:** This ruling required CMS to remedy the unlawful payment reduction. Hospitals have been litigating the remedy (repayment for prior periods) in subsequent proceedings.

### OPAIS System Updates
HRSA has periodically updated OPAIS to improve data accuracy and tracking. Covered entities must ensure their OPAIS data is current — any OPAIS outages or system transitions do not excuse late updates.

### Medicaid Managed Care and Duplicate Discounts
State Medicaid programs have increasingly moved to carve-in arrangements for MCOs. Covered entities must stay current with their state's approach and ensure their TPAs are configured accordingly.

## Monitoring for Updates

Because 340B guidance, legislation, and court decisions evolve frequently, certification candidates should:

- Monitor **HRSA's 340B website** (hrsa.gov/opa)
- Review **Federal Register** notices for proposed and final rules
- Follow **340B Health** and **NACHC** policy updates
- Track major federal court decisions affecting the program
