## The 2020-2021 Manufacturer Restriction Wave

Beginning in **mid-2020**, several major pharmaceutical manufacturers announced policies limiting 340B contract pharmacy access:

| Manufacturer | Restriction (as initially announced) |
|---|---|
| AstraZeneca | 340B pricing limited to one contract pharmacy unless the covered entity provides patient-level claims data |
| Sanofi | Similar data-sharing conditions |
| Eli Lilly | One contract pharmacy per covered entity without data sharing |
| Novo Nordisk | Similar limitations |
| Others | Varying approaches |

## HRSA's Response

HRSA took a firm position that these restrictions violate the 340B statute:

- Issued **advisory opinions** (2020-2021) stating manufacturers must offer 340B pricing for all covered outpatient drug purchases, including through contract pharmacies, without conditions
- Initiated the **civil monetary penalty (CMP) process** against AstraZeneca and others
- Argued that HRSA's authority under § 256b(d)(1)(B)(vi) permits CMPs for knowing and intentional denial of the 340B price

## The Litigation Landscape (as of 2024)

Federal courts have issued mixed rulings:

- **AstraZeneca Pharmaceuticals LP v. Becerra (D. Del., 2022):** District court initially ruled against HRSA's CMP authority; the Third Circuit reversed, finding HRSA had authority to enforce the statute
- **Eli Lilly v. HHS / Novartis v. HHS:** Courts have reached varying conclusions on whether the statute requires manufacturers to offer 340B pricing through unlimited contract pharmacy channels

> **Study note:** The litigation landscape continues to evolve. Always check for the most recent court decisions and HRSA guidance when advising on this area.

## Patient Data Demands

HRSA has issued guidance stating that manufacturers cannot **condition** 340B pricing on disclosure of patient-level data. Requiring patient names or prescription details as a precondition for 340B access is itself a violation.

## Covered Entity Practical Response

While litigation continues, covered entities should:
1. Document all manufacturer communications regarding pricing restrictions
2. Report manufacturer restriction notices to HRSA through established channels
3. Consult legal counsel familiar with the current litigation landscape
4. Monitor HRSA guidance and court decisions for developments
