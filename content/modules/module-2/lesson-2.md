## What Is Drug Diversion?

**Drug diversion** occurs when a covered entity uses 340B-purchased drugs for individuals who are **not eligible 340B patients**.

The 340B statute (42 U.S.C. § 256b(a)(5)(B)) explicitly prohibits covered entities from reselling or transferring 340B drugs to individuals other than their patients.

## Examples of Diversion

- Dispensing 340B drugs at a contract pharmacy to a walk-in customer with no established relationship with the covered entity
- Using 340B-purchased drugs for inpatient care (inpatient drugs are excluded)
- Providing 340B drugs to employees for personal conditions not treated at the covered entity
- Selling 340B drugs to another entity or redistributing them outside the program

## Consequences of Diversion

If HRSA determines a covered entity has engaged in diversion:

1. The covered entity must **repay the manufacturer** the amount of the 340B discount received on diverted drugs
2. The covered entity may be **removed from the 340B program**
3. Findings become part of the covered entity's compliance record

## Prevention Controls

### Split-Billing Software

**Split-billing software** (also called **inventory management software**) is the primary tool covered entities use to prevent diversion. It:

- Identifies which prescriptions are eligible for 340B pricing based on patient eligibility rules
- **Separates 340B purchases from non-340B purchases** at the point of dispensing
- Creates an audit trail for compliance documentation
- Prevents 340B drugs from being purchased for non-eligible patients

### Patient Eligibility Verification

At the point of dispensing, the covered entity (or its TPA) must be able to confirm:

- The patient has an **established relationship** with the covered entity
- The drug is a **covered outpatient drug**
- The drug is for the **patient's treatment** (not for resale or transfer)

### Audit Trail Documentation

Covered entities must maintain records sufficient to demonstrate — during an audit — that every 340B drug was dispensed to an eligible patient. This includes:

- Patient encounter records
- Prescription records
- Inventory tracking reports from the split-billing system
