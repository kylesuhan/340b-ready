## Why Self-Audits Matter

HRSA expects covered entities to maintain an **internal compliance program** that includes regular self-audits. Proactive self-monitoring:

- Detects compliance gaps before HRSA finds them
- Demonstrates good faith and program commitment
- Provides documentation showing active oversight
- Reduces the risk of material violations escalating

## Recommended Self-Audit Frequency

Industry best practices suggest:
- **Quarterly** patient eligibility spot-checks at contract pharmacies
- **Annual** comprehensive internal audit covering all five HRSA audit areas
- **Ongoing** monitoring via TPA/split-billing software reports

## Self-Audit Checklist

A comprehensive 340B self-audit should cover:

**OPAIS Accuracy**
- [ ] All pharmacy locations are current
- [ ] Authorizing Official and Primary Contact are current
- [ ] All child sites are registered before purchasing begins
- [ ] Re-enrollment is completed annually

**Patient Eligibility**
- [ ] Policies defining 340B patient criteria are documented
- [ ] Sample prescriptions at contract pharmacies have matching patient records
- [ ] Patients with lapsed relationships are not receiving 340B drugs

**Diversion Prevention**
- [ ] Split-billing/TPA software is operational and correctly configured
- [ ] Dispensing logs are reviewed against patient eligibility records
- [ ] Inpatient drugs are not purchased at 340B prices

**Duplicate Discount Prevention**
- [ ] Medicaid FFS carve-out or carve-in controls are documented and functioning
- [ ] Medicaid MCO controls are in place if applicable
- [ ] No 340B drugs are billed to Medicaid without appropriate tracking

**Contract Pharmacy Compliance**
- [ ] Written agreements are current and include required provisions
- [ ] TPA reports are reviewed regularly
- [ ] Contract pharmacy performance is monitored

## Corrective Action Plans (CAPs)

When a self-audit (or HRSA audit) identifies a compliance gap:

1. **Document the finding** — What specifically went wrong? How many transactions? What time period?
2. **Quantify the exposure** — Calculate the 340B discount received on non-compliant transactions
3. **Determine root cause** — Why did the failure occur?
4. **Implement corrective measures** — Change processes, retrain staff, update software rules
5. **Consider voluntary repayment** — For diversion findings, voluntary repayment to manufacturers is best practice and demonstrates good faith
6. **Monitor the fix** — Verify the corrective measures are working

> **Self-disclosure to HRSA:** Proactive disclosure of compliance failures, before HRSA discovers them, is recognized best practice and typically results in more favorable resolution.
