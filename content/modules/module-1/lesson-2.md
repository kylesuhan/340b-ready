## Who Are Covered Entities?

A **covered entity** is a federally-defined type of healthcare organization that is eligible to participate in the 340B program. The categories of covered entities are defined in **42 U.S.C. § 256b(a)(4)**.

## Hospital Types

| Entity Type | Key Requirement |
|---|---|
| Disproportionate Share Hospital (DSH) | Medicare DSH adjustment percentage > 11.75% |
| Children's Hospital | Exempt from DSH percentage requirement |
| Free-standing Cancer Hospital | Designated under Medicare rules |
| Rural Referral Center | CMS-designated |
| Sole Community Hospital | CMS-designated |
| Critical Access Hospital | CMS-designated |

> **Note:** Children's hospitals, cancer hospitals, critical access hospitals, rural referral centers, and sole community hospitals face the **orphan drug exclusion** under the ACA — they cannot use 340B pricing for orphan drugs.

## Non-Hospital Entity Types (Grantee Entities)

These entities are often called **grantee covered entities** because their eligibility is linked to specific federal grant programs:

- **Federally Qualified Health Centers (FQHCs)** — Health Center Program grantees (Section 330)
- **FQHC Look-Alikes** — Meet FQHC criteria but do not receive a grant
- **Ryan White HIV/AIDS Program grantees** — Titles I, II, III, IV, and V
- **State AIDS Drug Assistance Program (ADAP) grantees**
- **Maternal and Child Health Bureau grantees** (Title V)
- **STD/TB Clinics** (funded by Section 318 or 317 grants)
- **Hemophilia Treatment Centers**
- **Black Lung Clinics**
- **Urban Indian Organizations**
- **Native Hawaiian Health Centers**

## Eligibility Criteria — The Core Requirements

To be eligible, an organization must:

1. **Qualify as a recognized covered entity type** under the statute
2. **Receive certain federal grants or meet specific criteria** (varies by entity type)
3. **Register in OPAIS** (the HRSA database for covered entities)
4. **Comply with program requirements** including prohibitions on diversion and duplicate discounts

## Losing Eligibility

A covered entity may lose eligibility if it:

- Loses its qualifying grant funding
- Falls below the DSH threshold (for DSH hospitals)
- Fails to re-certify annually in OPAIS
- Is removed by HRSA for program violations
