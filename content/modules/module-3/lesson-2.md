## Overview of HRSA's Audit Authority

HRSA has statutory authority to audit both **covered entities** and **manufacturers** participating in the 340B program.

Audits of covered entities focus on five core compliance areas:

1. **Patient eligibility** — Can the entity document patients meet the three-part patient definition?
2. **Prevention of diversion** — Are 340B drugs only dispensed to eligible patients?
3. **Prevention of duplicate discounts** — Is the entity properly preventing Medicaid rebate overlap?
4. **Contract pharmacy compliance** — Are contract pharmacy arrangements properly documented and managed?
5. **OPAIS accuracy** — Is the entity's OPAIS information current and correct?

## Types of Audits

### On-Site Audits
HRSA conducts on-site audits at covered entity locations. Auditors review:
- OPAIS registration records
- Written policies and procedures
- Sample patient records to verify eligibility
- Prescription records and split-billing/TPA reports
- Medicaid billing records
- Contract pharmacy agreements

### Desk Audits
HRSA may request documentation for review without visiting the site. This typically involves submitting records electronically.

## Audit Selection

HRSA selects entities for audit through:
- **Random selection** — any covered entity may be selected
- **Complaint-driven** — manufacturer or other party files a complaint
- **Data anomaly** — HRSA's analysis identifies unusual purchasing patterns

> There is no size or voluntary compliance history exemption. Any covered entity can be audited.

## Audit Findings

HRSA audit findings are categorized by severity:

| Finding Type | Description |
|---|---|
| Material violation | Confirmed diversion or duplicate discount |
| Deficiency | Documentation gap, OPAIS inaccuracy, policy weakness |
| Recommendation | Suggested improvement, not a violation finding |

## Audit Response Timeline

Covered entities typically have **30 days** to respond to initial audit findings and **60 days** to submit a corrective action plan (CAP) if required.
