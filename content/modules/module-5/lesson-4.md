## OIG 340B Oversight

The **Office of Inspector General (OIG)** has published numerous reports examining 340B compliance. Key findings from OIG studies include:

### Patient Definition Documentation (OEI-05-13-00431)
A foundational OIG report found that a significant percentage of covered entities could not document that patients receiving 340B drugs at contract pharmacies met the HRSA patient definition.

**Key finding:** Inability to demonstrate established patient relationships at contract pharmacies is the most fundamental compliance gap.

### Hospital Outpatient Department Compliance
OIG has found that hospital outpatient departments (including those registered as child sites) sometimes failed to:
- Verify patient eligibility before dispensing 340B drugs
- Maintain adequate records linking prescriptions to patient encounters

### Manufacturer Ceiling Price Compliance
OIG audits have found instances where manufacturers charged covered entities above the 340B ceiling price, typically due to:
- AMP reporting errors
- Implementation delays in price updates
- Calculation errors at the NDC level

## Congressional Scrutiny

Congress has conducted hearings examining whether large health systems use 340B savings in ways consistent with the program's safety-net mission. Key questions:
- Do covered entities provide discounts on 340B drugs to uninsured patients?
- Do they report how 340B savings are used?
- Are child site registrations consistent with the entity's patient population?

## Current Enforcement Priorities

As of 2024, HRSA enforcement priorities include:
1. **Manufacturer contract pharmacy restrictions** — ongoing CMP proceedings and litigation
2. **Patient definition documentation** — continued audit focus
3. **OPAIS accuracy** — ensuring child site and contract pharmacy information is current
4. **Duplicate discount prevention in MCO carve-in states** — as more states move to carve-in
