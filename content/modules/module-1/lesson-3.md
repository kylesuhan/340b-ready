## What Is a Covered Outpatient Drug?

The 340B program only applies to **covered outpatient drugs** — a term defined in the Social Security Act (which the 340B statute cross-references).

### Definition

A covered outpatient drug is generally:

- An **FDA-approved** prescription drug
- A **biological product** licensed by the FDA
- An **insulin** product
- That is **dispensed on an outpatient basis**

### Key Point: Outpatient vs. Inpatient

The 340B program applies **only to outpatient drugs**. Drugs administered to inpatients as part of an inpatient hospital stay — and reimbursed under a bundled payment or DRG — are **excluded**.

## What Is NOT a Covered Outpatient Drug?

| Excluded Category | Reason |
|---|---|
| **Inpatient drugs** | Not dispensed on an outpatient basis |
| **Over-the-counter (OTC) medications** | Not prescription-required |
| **DESI-ineffective drugs** | Found less than effective by FDA's Drug Efficacy Study Implementation |
| **Orphan drugs (for certain entity types)** | ACA exclusion for specific hospital types |

## The Orphan Drug Exclusion

Under the **Affordable Care Act (ACA)**, certain covered entity types **cannot** use 340B pricing for orphan drugs:

- Children's Hospitals
- Free-standing Cancer Hospitals
- Critical Access Hospitals
- Rural Referral Centers
- Sole Community Hospitals

> **Note:** FQHCs, Ryan White grantees, and other non-hospital grantee entities are **not** subject to the orphan drug exclusion.

## Why This Matters

Ensuring that a covered entity only purchases **eligible drugs for eligible patients** is the foundation of 340B compliance. Purchasing a non-covered drug at 340B prices or purchasing drugs for inpatient use at 340B prices would constitute a compliance violation.
