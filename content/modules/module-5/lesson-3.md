## 340B and Medicare Part B

### The ASP+6% Model
Hospital outpatient departments are generally reimbursed by Medicare Part B for separately payable drugs at **ASP+6%** (Average Sales Price plus 6%).

When a covered entity purchases a drug at the 340B ceiling price and bills Medicare Part B at ASP+6%, the **spread** (difference) is a legitimate margin that covered entities can use to support safety-net care for uninsured and underinsured patients.

This spread is an **intentional feature** of the 340B program — not a loophole. It allows covered entities to generate resources that fund services for vulnerable populations.

### The CMS Rate Cut and AHA v. Becerra

In 2018, CMS cut the Medicare Part B reimbursement rate for 340B hospitals from ASP+6% to **ASP-22.5%**, arguing this better reflected covered entities' actual acquisition costs.

The Supreme Court's **AHA v. Becerra (2022)** decision ruled this cut unlawful because CMS did not conduct the required hospital acquisition cost survey before changing the rate.

> **Exam point:** The Supreme Court decided this case **unanimously** and focused on the statutory requirement to survey acquisition costs before changing the reimbursement rate.

## 340B and Medicaid Managed Care — Advanced Analysis

### The Duplicate Discount Problem in MCO States

When a state moves to a **carve-in** Medicaid MCO arrangement:
- The MCO pays the pharmacy for dispensing
- The state collects manufacturer rebates on those MCO claims
- If the covered entity also purchased the drug at 340B price, a **duplicate discount** occurs

### Practical Prevention Steps

1. **Configure the TPA** to flag MCO-billed 340B claims
2. **Coordinate with the state Medicaid agency** to ensure the exclusion file is properly maintained
3. **Audit** MCO billing records quarterly to confirm no overlap
4. **Document** the carve-in/carve-out decision and the controls in place

### State Variability

Medicaid managed care approaches vary significantly by state. Covered entities operating in multiple states must understand each state's approach and ensure state-specific compliance controls are implemented.
