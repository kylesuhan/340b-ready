## How to Approach 340B Certification Exams

### Core Areas Always Tested

Based on the structure of ACPE and similar 340B certification programs, expect questions in these areas:

1. **Statutory foundations** — What year, what statute, what purpose
2. **Covered entity eligibility** — Types, requirements, DSH threshold
3. **Patient definition** — Three-part test, edge cases
4. **Covered outpatient drugs** — Eligibility, exclusions, orphan drug exception
5. **Duplicate discounts** — Definition, Medicaid FFS vs. MCO, carve-in/out
6. **Drug diversion** — Definition, prevention, consequences
7. **OPAIS** — Registration, 10-day rule, annual re-enrollment
8. **Contract pharmacies** — 2010 guidance, written agreement requirements, TPA role
9. **Ceiling price** — AMP - URA formula, penny pricing
10. **ADR** — Who can file, process, panel structure
11. **Audits** — Selection, scope, audit findings, CAPs
12. **Recent developments** — Contract pharmacy restrictions, AHA v. Becerra

### Study Strategy

**Layer 1 — Get the definitions right**
- Every term has a precise meaning in 340B. Diversion, duplicate discount, covered entity, covered outpatient drug, OPAIS — know the exact definitions.

**Layer 2 — Apply the rules**
- Scenario questions test whether you can apply definitions correctly. Practice asking: "Does this patient meet the three-part test?" "Is this drug a covered outpatient drug?" "Would this create a duplicate discount?"

**Layer 3 — Synthesize across rules**
- Expert-level questions combine multiple compliance areas. A single scenario may implicate patient definition, diversion risk, AND duplicate discount risk simultaneously.

### Common Exam Traps

- **Telehealth:** Don't assume physical presence is required — it isn't explicitly
- **Orphan drugs:** Only certain hospital types face this exclusion — FQHCs and Ryan White do not
- **Carve-in vs. carve-out:** Know which direction increases vs. decreases duplicate discount risk
- **ADR filing party:** Both covered entities AND manufacturers can file
- **AHA v. Becerra:** The issue was CMS not surveying acquisition costs — not whether 340B hospitals could bill Medicare Part B
