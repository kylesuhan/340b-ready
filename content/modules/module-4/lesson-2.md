## The Role of a TPA

A **Third-Party Administrator (TPA)** in the 340B context is a technology and services company that helps covered entities manage compliance at contract pharmacies.

Key TPA functions include:

1. **Patient eligibility tracking** — Cross-referencing prescription data against the covered entity's patient records
2. **Split-billing / virtual inventory management** — Tracking which prescriptions are 340B-eligible and ensuring only eligible prescriptions result in 340B purchases
3. **Medicaid duplicate discount prevention** — Flagging 340B transactions that should be excluded from Medicaid rebate billing
4. **Replenishment ordering** — After eligibility is confirmed, triggering the covered entity's wholesaler account to order 340B-priced drugs to replenish what was dispensed
5. **Reporting** — Generating audit-ready reports showing compliance with patient eligibility and diversion prevention rules

## The Replenishment Model

The dominant model at contract pharmacies is the **replenishment (ship-to/bill-to) model**:

1. The **contract pharmacy** dispenses a drug from its own (non-340B) inventory to the patient
2. The **TPA** analyzes the transaction and determines if the patient meets the 340B patient definition
3. If eligible, the **covered entity** purchases an equivalent 340B-priced unit from its wholesaler to replenish the pharmacy's inventory
4. The replenishment is tracked at the NDC level

This model avoids the covered entity having to maintain separate physical 340B inventory at the pharmacy — a key operational benefit.

## Covered Entity Accountability

Using a TPA does **not** transfer compliance liability. The covered entity remains fully accountable for:
- The accuracy of patient eligibility determinations
- The prevention of diversion and duplicate discounts
- The completeness of records available for HRSA audit

> **Critical principle:** A covered entity cannot blame a TPA failure for a compliance violation in a HRSA audit. The entity must supervise and audit its TPA.

## Evaluating and Monitoring TPAs

Best practices:
- Review TPA reports at least monthly
- Conduct annual review of TPA contract and data security practices
- Ensure the TPA agreement includes audit rights for the covered entity
- Test patient eligibility rules periodically with known test cases
