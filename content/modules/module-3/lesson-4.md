## Manufacturer Obligations

Pharmaceutical manufacturers that participate in the **Medicaid Drug Rebate Program (MDRP)** are required, as a condition of that participation, to sell covered outpatient drugs to covered entities at or below the **340B ceiling price**.

This linkage is established at **42 U.S.C. § 256b(a)(1)**: manufacturers sign a Pharmaceutical Pricing Agreement (PPA) with the Secretary of HHS that includes the obligation to offer 340B pricing.

## The 340B Ceiling Price Formula

The ceiling price is calculated as:

> **340B Ceiling Price = AMP − URA**

Where:
- **AMP** = Average Manufacturer Price (reported quarterly by manufacturers to CMS under MDRP)
- **URA** = Unit Rebate Amount (the per-unit Medicaid rebate amount under the MDRP formula)

### Penny Pricing

When the AMP − URA formula produces a result **≤ $0.00**, the ceiling price is set at **$0.01** (one cent). This is called **penny pricing**.

## Why the Formula Is Linked to Medicaid

The 340B ceiling price is directly derived from Medicaid rebate data. This ensures 340B prices are at least as favorable as what Medicaid receives — a core legislative intent of the program.

AMP figures are confidential (reported to CMS, not published), so covered entities typically verify pricing through the **Prime Vendor Program** or work with their wholesaler.

## The Prime Vendor Program (PVP)

HRSA contracts with **Apexus** to operate the Prime Vendor Program. Apexus:
- Negotiates **sub-ceiling prices** (below the statutory ceiling) for covered entities
- Provides compliance resources, training, and audit preparation tools
- Operates a pricing verification tool for covered entities

## Manufacturer Overcharges

When a covered entity believes it was charged more than the 340B ceiling price:

1. **Contact the manufacturer directly** for a refund or credit
2. If unresolved: file a complaint through **HRSA's ADR process**

Manufacturers that knowingly and intentionally overcharge covered entities are subject to **civil monetary penalties of up to $5,000 per instance** of overcharging, plus required repayment.
