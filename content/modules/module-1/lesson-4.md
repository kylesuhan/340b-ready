## HRSA and the Office of Pharmacy Affairs

The **Health Resources and Services Administration (HRSA)** administers the 340B program through its **Office of Pharmacy Affairs (OPA)**.

HRSA's responsibilities include:

- Maintaining the **OPAIS** database of registered covered entities
- Establishing program requirements and guidance
- Conducting **audits** of covered entities and manufacturers
- Resolving **disputes** through the Alternative Dispute Resolution (ADR) process
- Imposing **civil monetary penalties** for violations

## What Is OPAIS?

**OPAIS** stands for the **Office of Pharmacy Affairs Information System**.

OPAIS is the HRSA-maintained database where:

- Covered entities **register** to participate in the 340B program
- Covered entities maintain their **program information** (authorizing officials, pharmacy locations, contract pharmacies, child sites)
- **Manufacturers and wholesalers** can verify that a covered entity is registered before selling at 340B prices
- The public can view registered covered entity information

## OPAIS Registration Requirements

To participate in 340B, a covered entity must:

1. **Submit a registration application** in OPAIS
2. **Designate an Authorizing Official (AO)** — a senior official who certifies program compliance
3. **Designate a Primary Contact (PC)** — the day-to-day compliance contact
4. **Register all participating pharmacy locations**, including contract pharmacies and child sites
5. **Re-certify participation annually** during the HRSA re-enrollment period

## OPAIS Update Obligations

Covered entities must update OPAIS **within 10 business days** of any material change, including:

- Adding or removing a contract pharmacy
- Adding or removing a child site (outpatient facility)
- Changes to the Authorizing Official or Primary Contact
- Changes in physical address

> **Important:** Failure to keep OPAIS current is itself a compliance violation and can lead to audit findings.

## The Compliance Certification

Each year, the Authorizing Official certifies in OPAIS that the covered entity:

- Has not knowingly permitted diversion of 340B drugs to ineligible individuals
- Has not knowingly caused duplicate discounts on 340B drugs
- Will comply with all program requirements

This certification is a binding legal representation.
