## Why Scenarios Matter

The 340B exam tests not just recall of rules, but the ability to **apply** them in complex, real-world situations. This lesson covers the most commonly tested edge cases in patient definition and eligibility.

## Telehealth Patients

**Question:** Can a patient seen via telehealth qualify as a 340B patient?

**Answer:** HRSA has indicated that telehealth encounters with a covered entity provider **can** satisfy the patient definition, provided:
- The covered entity provider has clinical responsibility for the patient's care
- The covered entity maintains health records for the patient
- The encounter is documented

This became particularly relevant during and after the COVID-19 public health emergency.

> **Key principle:** Physical presence at the covered entity is not explicitly required by the patient definition. The requirements are: established relationship, clinical responsibility, and records.

## Off-Campus Provider-Based Outpatient Departments (PBODs)

A hospital may operate multiple outpatient clinics located off the main campus. These are called **provider-based outpatient departments (PBODs)**.

**340B Rule:** A PBOD can serve as a source of 340B-eligible patients **only if** it is registered as a **child site** in OPAIS.

**Common Error:** A hospital registers a new PBOD and immediately begins purchasing 340B drugs for patients seen there — **before** the site is active in OPAIS. This is a violation.

## Employee Health Plans

Covered entities sometimes provide healthcare to their own employees. Can employees be 340B patients?

**Answer:** Generally yes, if:
- The employee is receiving care for a condition treated by the covered entity's providers
- The covered entity maintains health records for the employee as a patient
- The care is not merely occupational/workplace health (e.g., a required annual physical as a condition of employment)

Purely occupational health visits that do not create an established patient-provider relationship are typically not sufficient.

## Referred Patients

A community physician refers a patient to a covered entity's specialist. The patient has not yet had their first appointment.

**Can they receive 340B drugs?** Generally **no** — an established relationship requires an actual encounter, not just a referral. The patient must have been seen and have records maintained.

## Lapsed Relationships

A patient was seen at the covered entity 3 years ago but has not returned since.

**The answer is fact-specific.** HRSA has not set a bright-line rule. Covered entities should have policies defining when a patient relationship has lapsed, and should document those policies.
